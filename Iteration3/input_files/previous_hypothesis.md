The apparent tension between the GEMS and Sparkler populations arises because the atomic cooling model (Trenti et al. 2015) describes the formation of the *first* generation of halos, but fails to account for the *specific binding energy* of the clusters within those halos. I hypothesize that the observed metallicity offset and the "bifurcation" in formation epochs are explained by the **Halo Concentration-Metallicity Correlation**: GEMS clusters formed in high-concentration, early-collapsing halos (leading to rapid, high-density enrichment), while Sparkler clusters formed in lower-concentration halos that experienced delayed, stochastic enrichment. To test this, I propose mapping the observed [Z/H] and $z_{form}$ against the predicted halo concentration parameter ($c_{vir}$) for the respective formation redshifts. If the hypothesis holds, the GEMS and Sparkler populations will converge onto a single, continuous track in the [Z/H] vs. $c_{vir}$ plane, demonstrating that the "bifurcation" is a projection effect of the halo assembly history rather than a failure of the atomic cooling framework.
