**Title**: Quantifying the Atomic Cooling Threshold: A Comparative Epoch-Mapping of GEMS and Sparkler Globular Clusters

**Description**: This study evaluates the Trenti et al. (2015) atomic cooling model by mapping the observed GEMS ($z=9.625$) and Sparkler ($z=1.4$) globular cluster (GC) populations onto the predicted halo formation epoch. We calculate the formation redshift ($z_{form}$) for each cluster using Planck 2018 cosmology, utilizing the median ages to construct a discrete empirical probability distribution of formation epochs. To mitigate the significant age uncertainties in the Sparkler system, we employ a weighted analysis that prioritizes the GEMS cluster distribution as a high-precision anchor for the "cosmic dawn" formation peak. We conduct a statistical comparison between these median $z_{form}$ values and the theoretical peak of atomic cooling halo formation ($T_{vir} \geq 10^4$ K) to determine if both systems originate from the predicted halo mass regime. Finally, we perform a Spearman rank correlation between individual cluster metallicities ([Z/H]) and their respective $z_{form}$ values to test if the observed chemical enrichment gradients align with the hierarchical assembly timeline predicted by the LCDM framework. This approach provides a robust, falsifiable assessment of whether the atomic cooling threshold acts as a universal regulator for GC formation across disparate cosmic times.
